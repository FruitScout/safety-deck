The Risk
The Agave Field Is One of Agriculture's Most Dangerous Workplaces.
⚔️
Coa de Jima
Razor-sharp harvesting blade. Slipped coas cause severe lower-leg and foot lacerations.
🌵
Spines & Sap
Needle-like terminal spines. Highly acidic sap causes severe contact dermatitis and eye injury.
☠️
Agrochemicals
Lethal-dose pesticides require full respiratory and dermal protection. Field poisoning is a top grower liability.
☀️
Heat Stress
8-hour harvest shifts in full sun. Heat exhaustion is a significant and underreported risk.

Stages 2 & 3 · Chemical Safety Lock & Drone Monitoring
Every Spray Gated. Every Shift Monitored.

Chemical Application Safety Lock

  • Applicator scans chemical barcode before mixing. Label OCR retrieves exact PPE requirements from COFEPRIS database.
  • Worker takes a selfie. CV hard-gate verifies against the specific PPE demanded by that chemical — cloth mask vs. dual-cartridge respirator is caught every time.
  • System physically blocks "Start Application" timer and alerts agronomist if check fails.

Autonomous Mid-Shift Monitoring

  • While the drone flies routine inventory or phytosanitary scans, it automatically runs the PPE model on any workers captured in frame.
  • GPS-tagged violation heatmap — "Worker without Eye Protection at Row 47, Sector B3."
  • Weekly Safety Score per crew — tied to production bonuses, turning compliance into an incentive.
